# Business Context

## 1. Company Overview
The Company System is an internal web-based application designed for a medium-sized organization that operates with multiple departments and teams. The company manages several projects simultaneously and requires better coordination between departments.
Currently, operational processes rely on fragmented tools such as spreadsheets, emails, and messaging applications, which causes inefficiencies and lack of visibility.

## 2. Business Problems
The company is facing the following issues:
- No centralized platform to manage users, departments, projects, and tasks
- Difficulty tracking project and task progress
- Lack of clarity in roles and responsibilities
- Inefficient communication between teams
- Manual follow-up processes prone to errors
- No consolidated reporting for management

## 3. Business Objectives
The objectives of the Company System are:
- Centralize operational data in a single system
- Improve transparency and visibility across projects
- Clearly define roles and responsibilities
- Improve collaboration between departments
- Reduce manual follow-up and operational delays
- Support management decision-making with reliable data

## 4. Stakeholders
The stakeholders involved in the system include:
- **CEO / Top Management** – Strategic overview and decision-making
- **Department Heads** – Project and team supervision
- **Employees** – Task execution and updates
- **IT / Development Team** – System implementation and maintenance
- **Functional Analyst / QA** – Requirements definition, validation, and testing

## 5. Scope
- User and role management
- Department management
- Project and task management
- Task progress tracking
- Basic reporting and dashboards


## 6. Constraints
- Web-based solution accessible via browser
- Role-based access control
- Secure authentication and data protection
- Scalable architecture
- User-friendly interface

## 7. Assumptions
- Users have basic digital skills
- Internet access is available
- The system will be developed iteratively using Agile Scrum

## 8. Success Criteria
The project will be considered successful if:

- Projects and tasks can be tracked in real time
- Roles and responsibilities are clearly defined
- Manual follow-up processes are significantly reduced
- Users adopt the system with minimal training
